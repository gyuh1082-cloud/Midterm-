Inclusive Harlem Plaza

Redesigning Harlem Pedestrian Plaza for comfort, dignity, and shared public use.

Before vs After Transformation

About the Project

Project Goal: This project redesigns Harlem Plaza to improve comfort, accessibility, dignity, and shared use.

Essential Question:
How can Harlem Plaza be redesigned to feel more welcoming and usable for diverse users?

The Problem

Harlem Plaza currently lacks comfortable seating, shade, accessibility, and inclusive design. Many users cannot fully use the space due to limited amenities and poor layout.

Site / Context

User Groups:

  • Elderly residents
  • People with disabilities
  • Families
  • Commuters
  • Unhoused individuals

The site shows uneven comfort, unclear movement paths, and conflicts between different users.

Design Options

Comfort First Plaza

Focus on shaded seating, quiet areas, and rest zones.

Shared Use Plaza

Create flexible spaces for multiple activities and users.

Community Support Plaza

Design focused on dignity, inclusion, and social support.

Final Proposal

The final design introduces shaded seating, accessible pathways, and inclusive gathering areas. It supports a wide range of users while improving comfort, visibility, and shared use.
